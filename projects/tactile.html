Fabric-Embedded Resistive Soft Sensor for Touch Localization

Duration: June - Oct. 2025 Role: Researcher Lab: Hybrid Dynamics Robotics Lab (HDRL)
Soft Robotics Smart Textiles Tactile Sensing Low-Cost Sensors Sensor Fabrication Robotic Skin Research

Overview

Developed a low-cost fabric-embedded resistive soft sensor for contact localization that addresses the accessibility limitations of existing fabric-based sensing solutions. While fabric-embedded soft sensors are gaining popularity for robotic manipulation, therapeutic robots, smart textiles, and robotic skin applications, existing designs typically require expensive materials and specialized equipment, limiting their accessibility for research and education.

Key Features

  • Low-cost design: $1.14 per 100 cm²
  • Multi-touch detection across 25 sensing points, adjustable higher with analog expansion boards or DeMUX
  • Activation forces: 0.82 N for reliable contact detection
  • Response times: 81 ms for real-time applications
  • Repeatability: 25.6% variation within cells
  • Conformability: operates when wrapped around arms, fingers, and complex curved surfaces
  • Accessible manufacturing: requires only basic office equipment
  • Applications: robot manipulation, therapeutic robots, smart textiles, robotic skin, wearable devices

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Cost vs. Performance Trade-off
Existing high-performance fabric sensors require expensive materials or specialized equipment, creating a barrier to accessibility. We developed a solution using low-cost conductive fabric strips and polyester mesh, achieving competitive performance at lower costs.

Challenge 2: Manufacturing Accessibility
Many fabric sensor designs require industrial equipment or clean room facilities. We designed a manufacturing methodology that requires only basic office equipment.

Challenge 3: Conformability and Multi-Touch Detection
Achieving reliable multi-touch detection while maintaining conformability to complex surfaces is challenging. Our grid-based design with polyester mesh separation enables multi-point contact detection while maintaining flexibility for wrapping around curved surfaces.
